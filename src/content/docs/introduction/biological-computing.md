**Biological Computing** (or Biocomputing) involves using living biological components—like DNA, proteins, and cells—to perform computational tasks.

## How it Works

Instead of transistors and electrons, biological computers use:

- **Biomolecules**: DNA and RNA function as storage and logic gates.
- **Chemical Signals**: Hormones and proteins act as "wires" to transmit information between components.

## Key Frontiers

### 1. DNA Data Storage

DNA is incredibly dense and stable, capable of storing all the world's digital data in a few kilograms for thousands of years.

### 2. Living Sensors

Engineered bacteria can be programmed to detect specific toxins in the environment and "calculate" an appropriate response, such as changing color or releasing a neutralizing agent.

## Challenges

Biology is "noisy" and unpredictable compared to silicon. Developing reliable, programmable biological circuits requires advanced tools for DNA synthesis and CRISPR-based gene editing.
