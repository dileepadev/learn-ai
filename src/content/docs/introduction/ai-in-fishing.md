---
title: AI in Fishing
description: How artificial intelligence is transforming sustainable fishing practices.
---

Artificial Intelligence is making waves in the fishing industry, helping balance productive harvests with ocean conservation through smarter technology.

## Fish Stock Assessment

AI analyzes sonar data, catch records, and environmental conditions to estimate fish population sizes and distributions. Machine learning models process satellite ocean temperature and chlorophyll data to predict where fish aggregations will form. These assessments help regulators set sustainable quotas and help fishermen find productive fishing grounds.

## Sustainable Gear Optimization

Computer vision systems on trawls and nets identify catch species, returning undersized or protected fish while keeping target species. AI optimizes net design and towing patterns to maximize catch efficiency while minimizing bycatch. Some systems can sort catches in real-time, reducing handling time and stress on released fish.

## Fleet Management and Navigation

Machine learning integrates weather forecasts, fuel prices, and market conditions to optimize routing decisions. AI predicts where different species will be based on currents, temperature, and feeding patterns. Fleet tracking systems prevent overfishing in restricted areas and coordinate responses to emergencies.

## Processing and Quality Control

Automated sorting systems use AI to classify fish by species, size, and quality. Computer vision detects freshness indicators that human graders might miss. Processing line optimization reduces waste and ensures consistent product quality.

## Aquaculture Monitoring

In fish farms, AI monitors water quality, fish behavior, and feeding patterns. Machine learning detects early signs of disease or stress before visible symptoms appear. Automated feeding systems adjust rations based on growth rates and environmental conditions, improving efficiency while reducing environmental impact.
