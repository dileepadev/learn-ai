**Quantum Machine Learning (QML)** is the intersection of quantum computing and artificial intelligence. By leveraging quantum phenomena like superposition and entanglement, QML aims to process information in ways that are impossible for classical computers.

## How it Works

Instead of classical bits (0 or 1), QML uses **qubits**. Algorithms are designed to map classical data into a "quantum feature space" where complex patterns might be more easily separated.

## Potential Advantages

- **Exponential Speedup**: Certain algorithms, like those for linear systems of equations, could run exponentially faster on a quantum computer.
- **Model Capacity**: Quantum models may be able to represent complex probability distributions that are classically intractable.
- **Optimization**: Quantum Annealing and QAOA (Quantum Approximate Optimization Algorithm) can find near-optimal solutions for complex logistics and scheduling problems.

## Current Limitations

We are currently in the **NISQ (Noisy Intermediate-Scale Quantum)** era. Qubits are prone to errors (decoherence), and the number of available qubits is still too small for most large-scale AI applications.

## The Future

As quantum hardware improves, we expect to see QML revolutionizing areas like quantum chemistry, financial modeling, and extreme-scale pattern recognition.
