---
title: AI in Aquaculture
description: How AI improves fish farming through smart feeding, health monitoring, and water-quality prediction.
---

Artificial Intelligence is helping aquaculture operators raise healthier fish with fewer resources. By combining sensor data, computer vision, and predictive models, farms can improve yield while reducing waste and environmental impact.

## Smart Feeding and Growth Optimization

- **Feed timing optimization:** AI predicts when fish are most active to improve feed conversion ratios.
- **Portion control:** Models estimate biomass and adjust feed quantity in real time.
- **Growth forecasting:** ML predicts growth curves based on species, temperature, and nutrition history.

## Water Quality Intelligence

- **Real-time monitoring:** AI analyzes dissolved oxygen, pH, ammonia, and temperature trends.
- **Early risk detection:** Anomaly detection flags conditions that may trigger stress or mortality.
- **Proactive interventions:** Systems recommend aeration, water exchange, or stocking adjustments.

## Fish Health and Disease Detection

- **Computer vision monitoring:** Cameras detect abnormal swimming behavior and appetite changes.
- **Disease prediction:** AI correlates environmental shifts with historical outbreak patterns.
- **Targeted treatment planning:** Decision support helps prioritize treatment and quarantine actions.

## Sustainability and Operations

- **Energy optimization:** AI schedules pumps and aerators for efficiency.
- **Waste reduction:** Better feed control lowers nutrient runoff and water pollution.
- **Production planning:** Forecasting tools improve harvest timing and logistics coordination.

AI in aquaculture enables a shift from reactive farm management to data-driven, preventive operations.
