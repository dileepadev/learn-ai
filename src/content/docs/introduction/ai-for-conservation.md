title: "AI in Wildlife Conservation: Monitoring Earth's Biodiversity"
description: "How computer vision and acoustic sensors are being used to track endangered species and stop poaching."
---

Protecting biodiversity requires constant monitoring of vast, remote areas. **AI in Wildlife Conservation** provides "eyes and ears" on the ground to assist rangers and scientists.

## Core Technologies

- **Camera Traps**: Computer vision models (like YOLO or Megadetector) automatically identify animals in thousands of motion-triggered photos, filtering out empty shots.
- **Bioacoustics**: Deep learning models analyze audio from rainforests or oceans to identify specific species by their calls, even in noisy environments.
- **Satellite Tracking**: AI analyzes satellite imagery to detect illegal logging or habitat destruction in real-time.

## Stopping Poachers

Predictive models are being used to analyze historical poaching data and terrain to predict where poachers are likely to strike next, allowing for more effective ranger patrols.

## Case Study: Ocean Intelligence

AI is used to track whale migrations by analyzing the sounds they make, helping ships avoid collisions and allowing for the creation of protected marine corridors.
